  4. A detached condominium unit if the appraiser includes information about the project and its condition.

loanDepot requires that all appraisal reports comply with and adhere to the development and reporting requirements set forth by the Uniform Standards of Professional Appraisal Practice (USPAP), the Real Estate Settlement and Procedures Act (RESPA), the Financial Institutions Reform, Recovery, and Enforcement Act (FIRREA, and the Appraisal Independence Requirements (AIR) standards set by Dodd-Frank (H.R 4173).

Disasters

Note: reporting requirements are applicable when the subject property is included in an area identified by FEMA as a disaster area.

  • Appraiser to comment on any disasters that have occurred in the County of the subject property over the prior 120 days.
    • The appraiser is required to comment on any damage to the subject property and the subjects neighborhood caused by the disaster and to comment on any impact to marketability or value.
    • If applicable, appraiser to provide photos of any disaster damage. This includes damage to the subject property or the market area which can include - but is not limited to - damaged streets, power lines, utility systems, flooding, etc.
    • When possible, the appraisal should use sales that transpired after the disaster. However, if there are not sufficient post-disaster comparable sales or listings, then appraiser must provide current photos of the comparables. MLS photos or photos previously taken by appraiser prior to the disaster are unacceptable.
  • If the appraisal inspection was completed before a declared disaster, a property re-inspection by the appraiser may be required.
  • The re-inspection report must comment whether the property is safe, sound, and sanitary for the occupants and if the value of the property has been adversely impacted.
  • If re-inspection finds damage, then damage must be described and photographed, an estimated cost to cure provided, and an additional re-inspection requested to evidence completion of necessary repairs.

 

Land Subsidence & Sinkholes

  • The appraiser must analyze and report any readily observable conditions of the surface of the land that indicate potential problems. While not required to be an expert in the field of environmental hazards, the appraiser is responsible for noting any adverse conditions observed during the inspection or made aware of while performing due diligence. In the event of a sinkhole on the subject site, the appraiser must provide photographs of the impacted area.

 

Site and Zoning

  • The appraisal analysis must include any adverse effect that non-conforming use has on the value and marketability of the property.
  • An appraisal with illegal zoning compliance will not deliver through the FNC portal. see Assignment & Property Issues section above.
  • If site dimensions are not provided in the appraisal, the appraiser must provide the plat map and identify the subject parcel on the plat map.
  • No hypothetical lot sizes are allowed. The subject property is to be appraised according to legal records unless otherwise instructed.
    • If appraiser is provided a survey of site changes currently in progress, the appraiser must contact loanDepot for further instruction, for title may need to be revised and the survey recorded with the local governing authority.
  • Irregular lot shapes and any easements are to be noted in the report and on the plat map.

 

Multiple Parcels

  • Additional parcels may be included in the appraisal report.
  • An adjoining parcel may not have an additional dwelling unit.
  • Each parcel must be conveyed in its entirety, and all parcels must be contiguous (exception being if parcels are divided by a road and the divided parcel is non-buildable).
  • Each parcel must have the same basic zoning (for example, residential, agricultural).
  • All parcels encumbered in title are to be valued in their entirety, for the mortgage is a valid first lien that covers each parcel so that multiple parcels when encumbered - cannot be separated.

 

Highest & Best Use

  • The appraisers highest & best use analysis of the subject property should consider the property as it is improved. This treatment recognizes that the existing improvements should continue in the current use until it is financially feasible to remove the dwelling and build a new one or to renovate the existing dwelling. If the use of comparable sales demonstrates that the improvements are reasonably typical and compatible with market demand for the neighborhood, and the present improvements contribute to the value of the subject property so that its value is greater than the estimated vacant site value, the appraiser should consider the existing use as reasonable and report it as the highest and best use.
  • If the current use is other than the highest & best use, the appraiser must provide an analysis of highest & best use with supporting documentation.
  • If current and or highest & best use of the subject is other than residential, see Assignment & Property Issues section above.

 

Subject Property & Neighborhood Reporting Requirements

  • Appraiser must comment on the subject neighborhood or subject propertys proximity to adverse influences and address said factors with comparable sales that bracket the adverse features and provide market supported adjustments as necessary.
    • Photos of such factors, including but is not limited to busy streets, proximity to freeways/airports, commercial/industrial influences, etc. must be included in the addendum.
  • For properties with acreage or an agricultural zoning, appraiser must comment if they observed any commercial, agricultural/farming, or ranching activities, and provide details/photos of their observations. Where applicable, appraiser must identify and report if the subject property is being used as a working farm (income producing) or is considered a hobby farm.
  • For PUDs (Planned Unit Development), the following information is required:
    • Legal name of project.
    • HOA dues and assessments (if any).
    • Description of common elements and amenities for the subject and the comparable sales community.
    • When using non-PUD comparable sales provide an analysis to support the inclusion of non-PUD comparable sales.
  • Appraiser to adequately analyze and report any current contract of sale, option, offering, or listing of the subject property.
    • Appraiser to confirm that the most up-to-date purchase contract has been provided.
  • Report and analyze any prior sales of the subject property for three years prior to the effective date of the appraisal.
  • When subject has a prior sale within three years, appraiser to fully reconcile the prior sale price with the current opinion of value and detail any factors that have contributed to the difference.

 

Utilities and Off-Site Improvements

  • Properties with private site utilities require the appraiser to confirm that they meet community standards, note any readily observable deficiencies, and make the appraisal subject to any tests or inspections needed for verification.
    • If subjects water source is not public or a drilled well (private, community, or shared), appraiser to comment if the water source is common for the market and to demonstrate market acceptance by providing a comparable with a similar water source.
    • If subjects sewage-disposal system is not public, appraiser to comment if the sewage-disposal system is common for the market and to demonstrate market acceptance by providing a comparable with a similar sewage-disposal system.
  • If the subject is on a private road the appraiser must analyze and report the on the Road Maintenance Agreement details if available. If unavailable, the appraiser to comment whether there is adequate vehicular access and if there is an agreement of some type for vehicular access and maintenance.
  • If subject property is in a PUD, appraiser to comment if HOA maintains the private road.

     

    Improvements

  • If the subject has any unpermitted additions (including a garage conversion), comment on the quality and appearance of the work and the impact on marketability and value, on any functional obsolescence, and on any zoning violations.
  • Appraisal should not be made subject to permits.
  • If a primary dwelling has an additional manufactured home on site that is not valued as an Accessory Dwelling Unit, the manufactured home cannot be permanently affixed to the site and must be considered personal property with no contributory value given.

     

    Sketch/Floor Plan

  • Appraisers are required to use the ANSI Z765-2021 standard for measuring, calculating, and reporting gross living area (GLA) and non-GLA areas of subject properties for appraisals requiring interior and exterior inspections.
    • The ANSI standard must be followed for houses with detached or attached architectural design including townhouses, rowhouses, and other side-by-side houses.
    • Further details for ANSI Z765-2021 can be found at https://singlefamily.fanniemae.com/media/30266/display.
  • When unable to adhere to the ANSI Z765-2021 standard (and it is applicable), enter "GXX001-" at the beginning of the Additional Features field of the appraisal and provide an explanation of why unable to comply.
  • The building sketch must contain exterior dimensions and calculations for all the subjects levels (including basement) and for all outbuildings.
  • Any areas of functional obsolescence must be identified on the sketch including the use of interior walls.
  • Hand-drawn sketches are unacceptable.

     

    Photo Requirements

    • Any improvements with contributory value not captured in the front or rear photographs garages, outbuildings, etc.
  • Required interior photographs
    • Kitchen, main living area, all bathrooms, and all bedrooms
    • Basement

    Property Condition and Quality Reporting

  • Properties with Condition Ratings C1 through C5 are eligible for delivery in as is condition. For Properties with the initial Condition Rating of C6 and properties with one or more deficiencies that impact the safety, soundness, or the structural integrity of the property, the appraisal must be completed Subject-to the deficient item(s) being repaired or updated to bring the subject to a C5 or better condition.
  • Quality Ratings Q1 through Q5 are eligible for delivery. A property determined to be in Q6 condition may be eligible if the items noted do not affect the safety, soundness, or structural integrity of the property.
  • Appraiser must report and describe any deficiencies that could affect the safety, soundness, or structural integrity. If any items that are incomplete or the condition affects the safety, soundness, or structural integrity of the property, the property must be appraised Subject-to completion of the specific alterations or repairs and the condition and quality ratings based on the hypothetical condition that any noted items are cured or complete. If the appraiser is not qualified to evaluate the alterations or repairs needed, the appraisal must identify and describe the deficiencies and the property must be appraised subject to a satisfactory inspection by a qualified professional.
    • Any photos of potential health/safety issues must also include commentary indicating whether safety, soundness or structural integrity of the property is impacted.
    • Common issues that may require additional commentary:
      • Missing handrails on interior stairwells: as is report is acceptable if the absence does not pose a safety issue.
  • Missing guardrails: as is report is acceptable if the fall height does not pose a safety issue. Appraisal to specify the fall distance.
  • Security bars on windows: appraisal to comment whether windows with security bars in sleeping areas have safety release latches.
  • Water damage: appraisal to observe, report, and analyze the impact on marketability and value and to provide photos. Appraisal to comment on the source of the damage and if there are any active water leaks. Active leaks require a cure or a professional inspection evidencing no structural issue.
  • Mold-like growth: appraisal to observe, report, and analyze the impact on marketability and value and to provide photos. Lender will determine whether additional inspection or action is required.
  • Infestation/Dampness/Settlement: appraisal to observe, report, and analyze the impact on marketability and value and to provide photos. Either a cure or a professional inspection evidencing no structural issue is required.

     

    Sales Comparison Approach & Comparable Selection

  • Comparable Sales 1 through 3 must be closed sales, and all closed sales must have closed on or prior to the effective date of the appraisal.
  • Appraiser must adjust for the impact that concessions have on the sale prices per the definition of market value in all Fannie Mae/Freddie Mac appraisal forms. No adjustments are necessary for those costs which are normally paid by sellers as a result of tradition or law in a market area.
  • Include the following Comparable Sales list information within the appraisal report:
    • Description of the search criteria used (i.e., GLA, distance, time, age, etc.)
    • Explain the results of the search (i.e., GLA range, number of sales found, sales price range, etc.) then explain how the appraiser filtered the selection of sales.
  • loanDepot will accept an appraisal report that contains dated and/or distant sales. The appraiser must provide an analysis providing reasonable logic and rational for using sales that are dated and/or distant and define the search perimeters used in the search.
  • Unique / uncommon housing types
  • Appraisal to provide at least one sale of the same design and appeal to demonstrate impact on marketability and value. Appraiser may use dated or distant sales to meet the requirement.
  • If appraiser cannot locate a sale with the same design and appeal, appraisal is to provide sound support for any design and appeal adjustments (or lack thereof) to the comparable sales that are utilized.
  • Declining or Increasing Markets
  • Analyze and report the impact on value due to the declining or increasing market condition changes.
  • Provide a minimum of two (2) active listings or pending sales in the grid as part of the sales comparison approach.
  • Apply any necessary time adjustments along with support and analysis for the time adjustments or lack thereof.
  • Report and analyze any prior sales of the comparables within 12 months.
  • When an appraisal comparable has a prior sale within one year, appraiser to fully reconcile the prior sale price with the more current sale price and detail any factors that have contributed to the difference.

 

Accessory Dwelling Units

  • Only one ADU is allowed. If the subject has more than one ADU, please contact loanDepot for further instruction.
  • The ADU must be subordinate in size to the primary dwelling.
  • An ADU must be located on the same parcel as the main dwelling and have a means of ingress/egress, a kitchen, a sleeping area, a bathing area, and bathroom facilities.
    • The ADU may, but is not required to, include access to the primary dwelling. However, it is not considered an ADU if it can only be accessed through the primary dwelling, or the area is open to the primary dwelling with no expectation of privacy.
  • The construction method of an ADU can be site- or factory-built housing (including modular, and single- or multi-width manufactured homes) that are legally classified as real property. If an ADU is present, the primary dwelling must be site-built or a modular home.
    • A primary dwelling that is a manufactured home cannot have an ADU.

       

      ADU appraisal reporting requirements:

  • The ADU must be excluded from the Gross Living Area and must be valued as a separate line item in grid.
  • The ADU must be included on sketch
  • Include photos of interior rooms and, if applicable, exterior photos.
  • Appraiser must provide a minimum of one comparable sale to demonstrate market acceptance.
  • Appraisal must comment whether ADU is allowed per zoning.
  • ADUs may predate the adoption of the local zoning ordinance and therefore be classified as legal nonconforming.
  • An ADU should always be considered legal if it is allowed under the current zoning code for the subject property.
  • If the ADU violates zoning:
  1. The appraisal must comment that improvements do not comply with zoning.
  2. The appraisal report must demonstrate that the improvements are typical for the market through an analysis of at least two (2) comparable sales with the same non-compliant zoning use.

 

Second Kitchens

  • When the subject property has a second kitchen in the primary dwelling, Appraiser must comment:
    • If the second kitchen is allowed per zoning
    • If the second kitchen poses any health, safety, or structural integrity issues
    • On the quality and appearance of the work and its impact on the subjects marketability and/or value.
  • Appraisers should only observe, report, and analyze on the impact of the second kitchen. Appraisals should not be made subject to removal of the second kitchen. If a second kitchen violates zoning or if it poses a health and safety risk, that needs to be clearly stated within the report. loanDepots Credit Policy department will advise on how or if the issue can be cured.

 

Mixed Use Properties

A mixed-use property has a business use in addition to its residential use. The subject property must be primarily residential, and the borrower must be both the owner occupant and operator of the business.

 

Appraisal requirements for mixed-use properties must:

  • Provide a detailed description of the mixed-use characteristics of the subject property.
  • Indicate that the use of the property is a legal, permissible use of the property under local zoning requirements.
  • Report any adverse impact on marketability and market resistance to the commercial use of the property.
  • Report the market value of the property based on the residential characteristics rather than on the business use or any special business-use modifications.

 

New Construction Requirements

For all new construction appraisals, the appraiser must:

  • Review the most up-to-date executed purchase contract. A copy of the purchase contract is provided by loanDepot, but the appraiser is responsible to verify with the builder representative/agents that the contract they received includes the most current addendums and upgrades.
  • Discuss and analyze any lot premiums, options, and upgrades included in the sale price for the subject property and any builder comparable sales or listings.
  • Provide at least one settled comparable sale from the subjects subdivision or PUD, or condo project; If the subject property is part of a newly built or recently converted condo project, subdivision, or PUD that has 2-20 units and there are no settled or pending sales, the appraiser may use comparable sales from a competing project, subdivision, or PUD.
  • Provide at least one settled comparable sale from outside the subjects subdivision, PUD, or condo project.
  • If the subject property is not the first unit under contract in the condo project, subdivision, or PUD, the appraisal must include at least one other sale from the subject's project, subdivision, or PUD that is also under contract.
  • The appraisal must include a computer-generated sketch with exterior dimensions and area calculations.
  • If actual measurements cannot be obtained by appraiser, the dimensions may be taken from the blueprints or plans and specifications.
  • The floor plan must clearly identify all levels, rooms, bedrooms, bathrooms, and living areas (including basements, etc.).

For appraisals that are proposed construction or are made subject to completion, the appraiser must:

  • Review the plans and specifications for the subject and the project and include a statement in the appraisal that confirms that they were reviewed.
  • Include a copy of the specifications document as an addendum in the appraisal.
  • Include a materials/features list within the report that details the kitchen and bathroom materials (cabinetry, countertops, fixtures, etc.), floor coverings, interior doors, energy-efficiency items, siding material, etc.
  • If sufficient information is not available to the appraiser to assess subjects interior improvements, components, upgrades and build quality, a credible appraisal cannot be completed.  The appraiser should request that the appraisal assignment be placed on hold until the necessary information can be provided to the appraiser. The appraiser will request the necessary information or documentation from the loanDepot Appraisal Coordinator via AppraisalPort messaging.
  • The appraiser must inspect the subject property and will need to provide photos of the actual property from the front, rear, the street scene in both directions, and across the street from the subject.
  • When subject property is in the early phases of construction, the appraisal can be supplemented with interior/exterior photos of the builders model home, or another completed home of the same model if the photos are labeled in a manner that clearly identifies that they are not the subject property.
  • When possible, the appraisal should contain the builders name and the model/floor plan.
